Space Missions

Humanity's Greatest Journeys

From the first satellite to rovers on Mars — explore the missions that changed our understanding of the cosmos.

🔭

James Webb Space Telescope

Webb peers back over 13.6 billion years to capture the first light of the universe, revealing galaxies never before seen by human eyes.

Active
🚗

Perseverance Rover — Mars

Perseverance searches for signs of ancient microbial life on Mars and is the first mission to collect and cache Martian rock samples for future return.

Active
🛸

Voyager 1 — Interstellar Space

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is humanity's most distant spacecraft, now travelling through interstellar space over 23 billion kilometres from Earth.

Active
🌙

Artemis Program — Moon Return

NASA's Artemis program aims to land the first woman and first person of colour on the lunar surface, establishing a sustainable presence on the Moon.

Planned
🪐

Cassini-Huygens — Saturn

Cassini orbited Saturn for 13 years, revealing the complexity of its ring system and discovering geysers on the moon Enceladus that hint at subsurface oceans.

Completed 2017
☀️

Parker Solar Probe

Flying closer to the Sun than any spacecraft before, Parker is sampling the solar corona at speeds up to 692,000 km/h — the fastest human-made object ever.

Active
History of Space Exploration

Milestones in Space

Seven decades of reaching beyond our world — the moments that defined humanity's cosmic journey.

🛸
1957
Sputnik 1 — First Satellite
The Soviet Union launches Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, sparking the Space Race and proving objects could orbit Earth.
👨‍🚀
1961
Yuri Gagarin — First Human in Space
Gagarin completes one orbit of Earth aboard Vostok 1, becoming the first human to journey into outer space and return safely.
🌙
1969
Apollo 11 — Moon Landing
Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon. Over 600 million people watch live on television.
🚀
1998
International Space Station — Assembly Begins
The first module of the ISS is launched, beginning humanity's permanent presence in space. Today it houses 6–7 astronauts continuously.
🔭
2022
James Webb Space Telescope — First Images
Webb releases its first full-colour images, showing the deepest infrared image of the universe ever taken — galaxies from over 13 billion years ago.
